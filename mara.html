Mara was a two month long university project made with a group of 14 other students from different game development disciplines. Mara is a nightmarish Soulslike with a unique health system - Insanity. Getting hit by enemies increases one's Insanity, which leaves the player character more powerful, but also makes them more volatile than before.

I spent most of my time during this project creating levels and brainstorming ideas for the game’s health system. I was also in charge of writing the game design document. I wanted to create eerie experiences to go along with the theme of the game, and to achieve this I explored level design concepts like spatial empathy, as well as researched how emotions are developed through architecture.

A fascinating challenge I had to overcome in this project was creating mechanical penalties that were still fun to play with. Debuffs were a big part of the game through the Insanity system, but a lot of “conventional” debuffs would have simply made the player feel too penalized, so I had to find a way around that by creating debuffs that didn’t influence the gameplay experience negatively.
